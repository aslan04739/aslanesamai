In SEO, redemption is possible. You can improve your site quality, even if you have been a "naughty boy."

In the latest episode of Search off the Record, John Muller and Gary Ilyes discuss quality, how it is defined by Google, and most importantly, how SEOs can improve their content quality and overall site quality.

According to Gary Ilyes, websites can go out of crawl jail, as he calls it, and regain the trust of Google by following these steps:

Step 1: Have a Clear Definition

Have a clear definition of what low-quality content is according to Google.

Step 2: Delete Low-Quality Content

Delete thin and low-quality content from your site.

Step 3: Replace with Valuable Content

Replace the removed content with valuable and high-quality content that provides value to your users.

Step 4: Stay Focused

Stay focused on your long-term goals, don't lose your determination, and commit to providing quality content and user experience.
